Recent Papers (2023-2025) - Key Findings
Most Recent Methodological Advances:
1. Hey & Pavinato (2025) - "Isolating selective from non-selective forces using site frequency ratios"

Journal: PLoS Genetics
Innovation: Novel method using ratios of selected to neutral site frequencies to avoid confounding demographic effects
Key Advance: Works with Poisson ratios treated as Gaussian variables, avoids estimating relative mutation rates
Application: Successfully applied to Drosophila melanogaster populations

2. Crombie et al. (2024) - "Direct inference of the distribution of fitness effects from C. elegans mutation accumulation lines"

Journal: Genetics
Innovation: Uses recombinant inbred lines to reduce linkage disequilibrium confounding in DFE inference
Key Finding: Challenges conventional wisdom - suggests ~40% of mutations may have positive effects when linkage is not properly accounted for
Methodological insight: Demonstrates importance of controlling for linkage disequilibrium in DFE estimation

3. Soni, Pfeifer & Jensen (2024) - "Effects of mutation and recombination rate heterogeneity on DFE inference"

Journal: Genome Biology and Evolution
Focus: Impact of unmodeled rate heterogeneity on demographic and selective parameter inference
Tools Analyzed: DFE-alpha, Grapes, polyDFE, fastsimcoal2, δaδi
Key Warning: Rate heterogeneity can cause misleading inference if not properly modeled

Important 2022 Methodological Papers (Highly Relevant Context):
4. Ortega-Del Vecchyo et al. (2022) - "Haplotype-based inference of distribution of fitness effects"

Innovation: Uses haplotype identity-by-state lengths around low-frequency variants
Advantage: Can distinguish positive vs. negative selection in non-equilibrium populations
Application: UK10K dataset analysis

5. Dilber & Terhorst (2022) - "Robust detection of natural selection using probabilistic model of tree imbalance"

Innovation: Novel probabilistic model generalizing Kingman's coalescent for aberrant tree topologies
Robustness: Designed to be robust to population size fluctuations
Output: Frequency spectrum-based estimator as alternative to Tajima's D

6. Good (2022) - "Linkage disequilibrium between rare mutations"

Focus: Forward-time framework for predicting LD patterns under selection
Key Insight: Frequency scale dramatically impacts LD curve shapes
Relevance: Essential for understanding site frequency spectrum signatures

Key Trends in Recent Literature:

Methodological sophistication: Moving beyond simple summary statistics to model-based approaches
Demographic robustness: Increasing focus on methods that are robust to demographic misspecification
Linkage effects: Growing awareness of how linkage disequilibrium confounds DFE inference
Rate heterogeneity: Recognition that mutation/recombination rate variation affects inference
Multiple data types: Integration of different genomic signatures (SFS, LD, haplotypes)
