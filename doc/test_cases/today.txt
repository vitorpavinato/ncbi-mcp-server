Key Recent Methodological Advances (2023-2025):

Site Frequency Ratios Method (Hey & Pavinato 2025): Novel approach using ratios of selected to neutral sites to avoid demographic confounding effects when estimating DFE.
Accounting for Rate Heterogeneity (Soni et al. 2024): Demonstrates how unmodeled mutation and recombination rate variation can bias DFE inference, emphasizing need for better parameter modeling.
Linkage Disequilibrium Effects (Crombie et al. 2024): Uses recombinant inbred lines to show how LD between mutations can lead to overestimation of positive fitness effects in DFE inference.

These recent papers highlight ongoing challenges in DFE inference, particularly around demographic confounding, rate heterogeneity, and linkage effects, while proposing new methodological solutions.
